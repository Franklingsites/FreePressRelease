Free Specializing Press Release Writing Tips

Strong Headline

Keep it under 10 words and include the most important information.

Concise Content

Limit to 300-500 words and use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences).

Include Quotes

Add quotes from key stakeholders to add credibility and personality.

Add Links

Include relevant links to your website, products, or social media.

Lead with the News

Start with the most newsworthy detail — don’t bury the key announcement.

Use Timely Hooks

Connect your release to current trends, seasons, or events for added relevance.

Be Clear and Direct

Avoid jargon or fluff. Write like a journalist — simple, strong, and clear.

Know Your Audience

Write with your target reader or publication in mind. What would interest them?

Include Stats

Support claims with data or figures when possible to enhance credibility.

Stick to the Facts

Don’t exaggerate. A press release should inform, not advertise.

Add Media Assets

Enhance your release with a logo, image, or video to make it more appealing.

Include Contact Info

Always provide a media contact — name, email, and phone number if possible.
